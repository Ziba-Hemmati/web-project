About DNA

DNA, or Deoxyribonucleic Acid, is a molecule that carries genetic instructions:

  • Found in the cells of all living organisms
  • Consists of a double helix structure made up of nucleotides
  • Contains the genetic information that determines an organism's traits and characteristics
  • Plays a crucial role in inheritance and the passing of traits from one generation to the next
  • Used in various scientific fields, including genetics, forensics, and biotechnology

DNA is often referred to as the "building blocks of life" and is fundamental to understanding the complexity of living organisms.
