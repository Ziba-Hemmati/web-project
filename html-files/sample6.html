Mountains

Introduction

Mountains are majestic landforms that rise above the surrounding areas, typically characterized by steep slopes, high elevations, and rugged terrains. They are found on every continent and play a significant role in shaping the Earth's landscape.

Types of Mountains

Volcanic Mountains

Volcanic mountains are formed by the accumulation of volcanic materials, such as lava, ash, and pyroclastic debris. They are typically cone-shaped and can be found near volcanic hotspots or along tectonic plate boundaries. Mount Fuji in Japan and Mount Kilimanjaro in Tanzania are examples of volcanic mountains.

Highest Mountains

The highest mountains in the world are part of the Himalayan range. Mount Everest, standing at 8,848 meters (29,029 feet), is the tallest mountain on Earth. Other notable peaks in the Himalayas include K2, Kangchenjunga, and Makalu.
